Community 2.0 · Product & Build Plan

One community page that earns its real estate

Today the community lives in three disconnected scrolls — a buried wait-check, a huge empty "Find your visa community" grid, and a thin thread list. This plan collapses them into a single, engaging feed led by the thing people actually want: real timelines from real applicants, with the official figures as a quiet second source and discussion right below.

01 — THE PROBLEM

The page asks people to work for the value

Everything good is here already — the wait-check engine, official-vs-community processing times, spaces, threads. But it's laid out as a brochure, not a feed. The visitor has to scroll, switch tabs, and click into separate pages to assemble the picture themselves.

Three separate scrolls

Processing times, a full-page spaces grid, and discussions live in stacked sections. Timelines, official figures and community chatter never appear together.

Dead real estate

"Find Your Visa Community" is a 10-card grid where 9 of 10 spaces read 0 threads. It's the largest section and the emptiest — it makes the place feel abandoned.

Timelines are invisible

The richest signal — applicants' real milestone-by-milestone journeys — is hidden behind a single-span "Share your timeline" dialog and never rendered as content.

The wedge, in one line

People arrive asking "is my wait normal?" — the whole page should answer that on sight: here are people exactly like you, here's where you sit, here's the official number, here's what they're discussing. No tab-hunting.

02 — PRINCIPLES

What the redesign optimises for

Show, don't gate

Whole timeline cards and full thread previews render inline — no tap-to-expand. The visitor feels the activity in the first screen.

Community first, official second

Lead with what applicants are sharing. The official Home Affairs figures become a trusted-but-secondary reference strip, collapsible.

Density via horizontal scroll

Shorter, narrower timeline cards in a horizontal rail let people see six journeys at a glance instead of one tall block.

Contribute → belong

"Share your timeline" is pinned in the rail and ends in "you're part of this." Sharing posts to the feed and feeds the stats.

03 — THE NEW PAGE

From brochure to feed

Same ingredients, re-sequenced around the four priorities you set: (1) timelines people share, (2) encourage sharing, (3) official as secondary, (4) visa community + discussion.

Today
  1. Tall hero (50vh) — "Where Journeys Connect"
  2. Stats bar (10 / 1 / 1)
  3. Processing Times — wait-check + tabbed official/community board
  4. Find Your Visa Community — 10-card grid, mostly empty
  5. Recent Discussions — thread list + sidebar
Proposed
  1. Compact hero + inline wait-check — "Is my wait normal?" answered on screen one
  2. Official figures — collapsible secondary strip right under the hero, with community delta
  3. One community feed (PRIMARY, Facebook/IG-web shape) — sticky left rail to pick interest · single scrolling feed of timelines + questions · sticky right context rail
  4. Colorful inline timelines — timeline posts render a horizontal milestone strip (per-type coloured icons + day-gaps) right in the feed, no tap needed
  5. Tap any post → detail drawer on the right with the full coloured journey + a flat reply conversation

One feed, three columns — like Facebook / Instagram web

The two-pane "browser" (sidebar scroll + grid scroll) was two competing scrolls — tiring. Replaced with a single social-feed shell: a sticky left rail ("pick what you're interested in" — All posts · Questions · Timelines · the four visa families · Still waiting / Recently granted, each with a live count + a prominent Share button), a single center feed that owns the page scroll (one continuous list, like reels/posts), and a sticky right context rail (share prompt · "built to stay honest" · popular spaces). The left and right pin; only the feed scrolls — one scroll, not three.

One "post" model — timeline or question, shown richly

A shared timeline and a question are the same post type in the one feed. Question posts render title + body; timeline posts render a colourful horizontal milestone strip inline (each milestone a per-type coloured icon — Visa Lodged, Medical, S56, Granted… — with day-gap chips between), plus a wait-tier badge. Everything's visible without tapping; tapping opens the same detail drawer for the full journey + conversation. No separate "thread" concept to learn.

Detail drawer + flat conversation (no nested threads)

Tapping a post slides in a wide right drawer (~600px): the note, a scrollable "Application Journey" box (fixed height, all milestones in colour), a reactions row, then the conversation. Replies are deliberately one level deep — a message, replies under it shown flat with an OP tag, and replying to a reply appends to the same message's conversation. This is the "message → reply → reply-back" model the user wanted, not a branching Reddit tree.

05 — DATA MODEL

Journeys & milestones — plus the guardrails that keep it honest

Today's model can't express the new card

SubmitTimelineRequest stores a single lodged_on → decided_on span (good for percentile stats). The redesigned card shows an ordered list of milestones (Nomination Lodged → Visa Lodged → Medical → S56 → Granted) plus a profile (stream, occupation, state, sponsor). We need a richer record — without losing the existing stats pipeline.

Approach: add a journeys + journey_milestones pair, derive the span

Keep the existing percentile engine working by deriving the lodged/decided span from the milestones (lodged = first "Lodged" milestone, decided = "Granted/Refused" milestone). The legacy single-span timelines table can stay as the materialised stats source, populated from journeys — or journeys can replace it with a compatibility view. New columns are additive; no destructive migration.

Identity & anti-abuse — the guardrails

The system must be usable with no sign-in, but not abusable. The model: one anonymous identity per device, with hard one-time limits that convert into a sign-in prompt.

Auto identity, not free text

On first visit the server issues a unique handle (e.g. BoldLagoon7745) bound to a signed device token — shown in the nav and pre-filled when posting. Users can reroll the name before their first post, then it locks. No impersonation, no handle-squatting.

One vote per person

Votes are deduped server-side by UNIQUE(identity, target). Clicking again toggles off — it can never inflate a count. Fixes the "I could upvote 100 times" hole directly.

One timeline, then sign in

An anonymous identity may post a single timeline. After that, every Share CTA flips to a "you've shared ✓" / sign-in-to-do-more gate. Signing in lifts the cap and unlocks edit, reply notifications, and following a cohort.

Defence in depth, server-enforced (never client-trusted): device token + per-identity rate caps, optional lightweight challenge on post, and the existing report/moderation pipeline. Comments stay anonymous but are rate-limited per identity.

Privacy & moderation hold

Posts are anonymous (handle only — no name/email). Occupation + state + sponsor are coarse enough not to identify anyone. Reuse the existing s276/report + moderation pipeline; posts (timeline or question) and comments are reportable, and the same hide/remove actions apply.

08 — OPEN DECISIONS

Calls to make before Phase 3

Replace the legacy timelines table, or keep both?
Recommend: keep timelines as the stats source, populate it from journey-derived spans via a trigger/recompute. Lowest risk — the wait-check & processing endpoints keep working untouched.
Seed the empty feed, or launch sparse?
Recommend: seed a curated set of realistic journeys (clearly handled, never fabricated outcomes) so the rail never looks dead — consistent with the honest-sourcing stance already in the repo. Decide the labelling: "sample" vs real.
Are journeys their own entity, or a thread subtype?
Recommend: separate journeys entity unified at the feed level (timeline | question post type), reusing comments/votes/reports polymorphically. Cleaner stats derivation than overloading threads.
Device-token identity — how strict before it hurts real users?
Recommend: signed httpOnly cookie as the primary key, soft-backed by coarse fingerprint for cookie-clearers. One post + one-vote-per-target is enough friction without blocking shared/public devices. Escalate (challenge) only on burst abuse, never on the first action.
Flat conversation vs. allowing deeper threads later?
Decided (per feedback): one level only — message → replies, replies-to-replies flatten onto the parent with an @mention. Store parent_comment_id anyway so we could relax this later without a migration.
What exactly does signing in unlock?
Recommend: multiple/editable timelines, reply notifications, save-visa & follow-cohort. Keep reading and the first post fully anonymous so the funnel stays frictionless.
How much profile is too much (re-identification)?
Recommend: cap at visa · stream · occupation · state/area · sponsor. No DOB, no exact dates beyond milestones, no employer. Revisit if any combo gets too specific for rare occupations.
