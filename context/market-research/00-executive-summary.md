# IMMI-PULSE Market Research — Executive Summary

**Date:** April 2026
**Scope:** Immigration technology landscape across Australia, Canada, and the United States

---

## The Opportunity

The immigration technology market is fragmented across hundreds of single-purpose tools, aging forums, and country-specific platforms. No single product unifies the experience for either immigration professionals or applicants.

## Three Critical Findings

### 1. Australia Is Wide Open for AI Disruption

Migration Manager dominates the Australian consultant market (~2,000+ agents) but is **legacy desktop software with zero AI capabilities**. Meanwhile, every AI-powered immigration startup (Parley, Visalaw.ai, Gale, LegalOS, DraftyAI) is **US-only**. The ~7,000 OMARA-registered agents in Australia have essentially no AI tooling available to them.

### 2. Nobody Owns the "Full Journey" Experience

The market is completely siloed:
- **Tracking** → Lawfully, ImmiTracker
- **Community** → Reddit, Facebook, Telegram
- **News** → CIC News, VisaEnvoy, SBS
- **Consultant tools** → Docketwise, Migration Manager
- **Self-service** → Boundless (US marriage visas only)

An applicant uses 5-10 different platforms across their immigration journey. The platform that unifies this wins.

### 3. Universal Pain Points Remain Unsolved

Across every platform, users consistently report:
- "Where am I in the queue?" anxiety with no real visibility
- Unverified advice from strangers
- Information scattered across too many platforms
- Poor mobile experiences (forums are desktop-era)
- Spam and fake agents in Facebook/Telegram groups
- No emotional support during long processing waits
- No cross-country comparison tools
