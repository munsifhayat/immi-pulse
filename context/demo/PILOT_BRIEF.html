02 · The pipeline, in four stages

From query to paying client, in plain view.

Each stage has one job. A clear gate moves work to the next. Nothing gets lost between sticky notes and inboxes.

01 · Inbox

A new query.

Someone fills your form. The submission lands here, AI-summarised, with a fit signal — likely fit, needs more info, paid consult, unlikely.

Move on by Marking qualified

02 · Pre-case

A qualified lead.

The lead is real. You compose an engagement letter from your template and send a secure link. Client signs in their browser — no DocuSign, no email back-and-forth.

Move on by Letter signed

03 · Pre-case

Awaiting retainer.

Bank transfer, PayID, BPAY, cash, cheque — when the money lands, you record it once. Sequential receipt number is generated automatically. Stripe slots in here later.

Move on by Payment received

04 · Case

An open matter.

One click converts. The checklist is generated, the client portal is minted, document collection begins. You're now doing the work you were trained to do.

04 · Built for real life

Auto by default. Manual when life happens.

Every gate has a polite trapdoor. Pro-bono cases, walk-ins, paper-signed agreements, cash retainers — the platform records the override, audits it, and keeps moving. You're never blocked.

  • Override · Inbox
    Open a case directly from a Client
    Walk-in client already engaged offline. Skip the entire ladder. The system records why.
  • Override · Letter
    Mark engagement letter signed manually
    Signed in person, on paper. You attest, the audit row is logged, the gate closes.
  • Override · Payment
    Skip payment for a relative case
    A zero-dollar waiver record is added with your reason. The pipeline keeps moving.
  • Override · Pipeline
    Force-convert to a case, with reason
    For the messy ones. Both the skipped letter and skipped payment are stamped in the audit trail.
05 · Quietly regulated, by design

Built around the code of conduct, not patched onto it.

The pipeline structurally enforces what regulators expect — not as a checklist, but as the shape of the work itself.

OMARA Code of Conduct
No assistance without a service agreement
Cases cannot open without a signed engagement letter and a recorded retainer (or an explicit, audited override).
Electronic Transactions Act 1999 (Cth)
E-signature legally binding
Every signature captures method, signer name, IP, browser, body SHA-256 hash, and consent text — exceeding ETA requirements.
Privacy Act 1988 + 2024 Reform
Reasonable steps, evidenced
TLS in transit, KMS-encrypted at rest in ap-southeast-2, PIN-protected client portal, full audit log on every read and write.
